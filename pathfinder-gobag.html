Incident War Room

A structured set of guides to keep analysts focused on finding the data necessary to track and contain the adversary.

ABCs of Incident Response

Framework by Mike Melone · Microsoft Defender Virtual Ninja Training · Map evidence across 3 timeframes · Watch the training video ↗

A
Authentication
What identities were used, stolen, or modified — including any account that launched a malicious process?
B
Back Doors
What mechanism(s) did the attacker use to control the endpoint?
C
Communication Channels
What sources & destinations were used to carry out the attack?
D
Data
What was the confidentiality, integrity, or availability impact of any data lost?
MIND Framework

Framework by Patterson Cake · securecake.com ↗ · Watch the Rapid Incident Response video ↗

M
Memory
Processes, services, and loaded modules running in memory at the time of the attack.
I
Identity
Compromised usernames, emails, accounts, and credentials leveraged by the attacker.
N
Network
IPs, domains, URLs, C2 channels, and exfiltration destinations observed in traffic.
D
Disk
File hashes, malicious files, persistence paths, and registry keys found on disk.
